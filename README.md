Smart Barcode
Increasing the efficiency of school processes
An ICT project with the potential to become more

Problem Statement: One of the more prominent problems in the school recognized by everyone is the way simple processes, such as cafeteria or washroom entry, are managed. There is a need to make such processes more efficient and at the same time more structured, a way to prevent non-meal seekers from entering the cafeteria behind the didi’s back, or students from ‘bunking’ by going to the washroom repetitively. 

Idea: 
On all our ID-cards is a barcode. This barcode identifies us and already allows us to scan and issue books from the library, but it can do so much more. 
The idea of the ‘Smart Barcode’ is to install similar barcode scanners in the cafeteria, washroom, and potentially all across the school.
We could develop a database to track the movement of the students in terms of how often they go to the washroom, and if made easily accessible, teachers could utilize this to prevent students from bunking.
The cafeteria is the most realistic application of the same, where we could test and eventually automate cafeteria entry.

Developing hardware to actually deny entry
